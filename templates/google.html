EXPLORATORY DATA ANALYSIS

Google LLC is an American multinational technology company that specializes in Internet-related services and products. Founded by Larry Page and Sergey Brin while they were Ph.D. students at Stanford University on September 4, 1998, Google has since become one of the world's most valuable companies. It is primarily known for its search engine, but it also offers a wide range of services and products, including advertising services, cloud computing, software, and hardware. Google's parent company, Alphabet Inc., was created through a corporate restructuring of Google on October 2, 2015. Google's stock is traded on the NASDAQ under the ticker symbols GOOGL and GOOG.

The close price chart plots the closing prices of an asset over a specified period, providing a clear visual representation of how the asset's price has changed over time. The closing price is the last price at which the asset traded during the trading period.

A moving average chart overlays the moving averages on the price chart of an asset. Moving averages are calculated by averaging the closing prices of the asset over a specified number of periods, providing a smoothed representation of price trends and helping to identify the direction and strength of the trend.

The candlestick chart is a popular financial chart used to represent price movements of a security, derivative, or currency over time. Each "candlestick" in the chart displays four key pieces of information: the open, high, low, and close prices for a specific time period (e.g., day, week, hour).
